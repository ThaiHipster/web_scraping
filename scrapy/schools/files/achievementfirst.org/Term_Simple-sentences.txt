Term: “Simple sentences”
Definition: A simple sentence, also called an independent clause, contains a subject and a verb, and it 
expresses a complete thought. Simple sentences can also contain a compound subject or verb.

Example

A. Some students like to study in the mornings.
B. Juan and Arturo play football every afternoon.
C. Alicia goes to the library and studies every day.

Analysis: Note that sentence B contains a compound subject, and sentence C contains a compound verb.
