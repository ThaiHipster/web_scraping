Term: “Reasonable Background”

Deﬁnition:
Information is provided to frame the overall argument so that a reader who is not familiar with the text or
content had a general sense about the people, events, or concepts explored in the written argument. At a level
four on the rubric, there may be blatant omissions or extraneous information included, but the author clearly
attempted to provide framing for the reader.
