Term: “Simple Sentences”

Deﬁnition:
A simple sentence, also called an independent clause, contains a subject and a verb, and it expresses a
complete thought. Simple sentences can also contain a compound subject or verb.
A. Juan and Arturo play football every afternoon. B. Alicia goes to the library and studies every day.
ANALYSIS: Note that sentence A contains a compound subject, and sentence B contains a compound verb,
but both are still simple sentences.
