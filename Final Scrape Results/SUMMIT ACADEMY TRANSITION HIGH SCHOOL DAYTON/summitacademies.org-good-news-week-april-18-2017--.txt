Good News – Week of April 18, 2017
Date Published:04/24/2017

On Tuesday, May 9, the Cincinnati High School choir and instrumentalists will perform at the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in Cleveland on its main stage.  The 45-minute performance will features songs from icons of rock throughout the decades.  The group will perform hit songs from the 60s, 70s, and 80s.  The performance is open to the public through general admission to the Hall of Fame.
_______
History buffs from Youngstown Secondary competed April 1 in a district competition, and two teams are moving on to state competition.  Congratulations to Karissa Zalovcik, Jamison Tubbs, and Marquan Stevenson.  Karissa placed first in the senior individual division, and Jamison and Marquan placed third in the senior team division.  Twenty-five schools participated.
State competition is slated for April 29 at 8 a.m. in the Merrick Hall Building at Ohio Wesleyan University.  Final judging is at 1 p.m., and the award ceremony begins at 4 p.m. at the Branch Rickey Physical Education Building.
_______
Summit Academy Community School for Alternative Learners Xenia will celebrate “Every Kid Healthy Week” April 24-28 with fruit and vegetable smoothies for breakfast on Monday.  Tuesday is class challenge day, where students will record how much physical activity classes get outside of specials.  Wednesday is obstacle course day, where students complete the obstacle course to receive a prize.  Thursday is fruit-and-vegetable kebob tasting, and Friday is a fun run/walk, where students, teachers, and parents will participate in a 1.5 mile fun run/walk.  Health facts and strengthening exercises are added to the run/walk, which ends with snail juice, flavored water and snacks.  The school’s goal also is to drink 76 gallons of water during the week.
_______
Summit Academy Painesville raised more than $7,000 for its PTST fund when it hosted its 7
th annual Pride Auction and Spaghetti Dinner on April 8 at the Fr. Hanzo Family Center.  Dinner was served at 5:30, followed by a Chinese auction and a 50/50 raffle.
_______
Akron Middle School’s Life Skills classed raised $411 to buy Easter holiday groceries for four families in need.  The students made items to sell in order to raise the money, and they planned meals, then traveled to Save-A-Lot to purchase groceries for the families.  Kudos for paying it forward!
_______
On April 13, Lorain Middle hosted comedian and motivational speaker Ricky Smith, who spoke to students about the importance of applying themselves in school.  Raised in Warrensville Heights, OH, Ricky entertains audiences with his “off-kilter” wit.  He also is a committed philanthropist, engaging in random acts of kindness.  Ricky established RAKE (Random Acts of Kindness Everywhere), a coalition of individuals who give back to the community in myriad ways.

About 
Summit Academy Management
Founded in 1999, Summit Academy Schools is a safe haven for children with learning disorders such as Autism, ADD, ADHD, and Asperger’s. Our Individualized Education Program, special services, highly qualified staff members, and our special features like Therapeutic Martial Arts and the DOJO room all work in cohesion to create a positive, encouraging, and helpful environment for our students to learn and flourish.
