​Larry McGill, Jr., School Leader
Larry McGill, Jr., a Phoenix resident, is the school leader of South Pointe High School in south-central Phoenix, a tuition-free Leona Group public charter school. Mr. McGill has served as school leader since South Pointe’s inception in 2003. Prior to assuming the role of school leader at South Pointe, Mr. McGill served as the school leader for three years at Leona's Desert Hills High School located in Gilbert, Arizona. Before that, he held the position of assistant school leader at Summit High School, another Leona sister school. Mr. McGill holds a bachelor's degree in psychology from Ottawa University and is a member of the National Association of Secondary School Principals and has been involved in education since 1995.  Mr. McGill has been a member of The Leona Group family since 1999.

​Virma Chavez, Assistant School Leader
Mrs. Chavez is our Assistant School Leader.  In the past, she has taught Spanish Levels 1-4, Criminal Justice, and Business Management.  She was also the Senior Class Counselor and Athletic Director.  She was born in Mexico, but raised in Fountain Hills, Arizona.  She graduated Magna Cum Laude from Arizona State University, where she earned a Bachelor of Science in Justice Studies and a Bachelor of Arts in Spanish.  She also earned a Masters in Business Administration (MBA) from the University of Phoenix.

Phuong Mai Pham, Curriculum Coach
Phuong Mai Pham is currently South Pointe High School's Curriculum & Instruction Coach. Her expertise is in working with students from the urban school systems. She taught middle and high school math for 5 years. She attended the University of Washington and is currently pursuing her M.Ed Leadership at NAU. Ms. Pham is extremely passionate about closing the achievement gap and is excited to be at SPHS. She taught in South Phoenix for 3 years and believes that this is where she truly belongs as an educator. 
​
"I am a firm believer that every student is able to learn and to be successful if given the correct tools, resources, and support. I hope to be able to give all three to our students and staff members."
